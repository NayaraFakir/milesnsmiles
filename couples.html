Cape Verde

Cape Verde is a volcanic archipelago situated in West Coast Africa. As it is close to the equatorial line, it is quite hot in the summer and humid in the winter. The counties official language is Portuguese, but they also speak Cape-Verdean creole (another dialect). Two of the most visited cities are Praia (the capital) and Santa Maria. If you're into volcanos you can visit Pico do Fogo: the highest point of the volcano on the Fogo island. However, if you prefer sun and calm, Chave's beach is the place to go. Their main dishes are Cachupa (a type of stew) and Canjo (a type of soup). In terms of accommodation, Hotel Riu Touareg (4.5 rating stars) and Hotel Morabezas (4.6 rating starts) are ideal for families.

Jordan

Known by its ancient attributes, Jordan is a country situated on the east bank of the Jordan River. Here you can explore Petra's Rock-Cut City in a deep valley or even visit Amman Citadel, an archaeological site with remnants of past civilisations amongst many other attractions. Jerash and Amman are Jordan's most visited cities enriched with history and culture. The climate consists of hot, long and dry summers and cool winters. They are known for their Mansaf (a lamb dish) and Maqluba (meat and rice type of dish). InterContinental Aqaba Hotel (4.5 rating stars) and Petra Palace Hotel (3.4 rating stars) are perfect to accommodate families.
