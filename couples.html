Cape Verde

Cape Verde is a volcanic archipelago situated in West Coast Africa. As it is close to the equatorial line, it is quite hot in the summer and humid in the winter. The counties official language is Portuguese, but they also speak Cape-Verdean creole (another dialect). Two of the most visited cities are Praia (the capital) and Santa Maria. If you're into volcanos you can visit Pico do Fogo: the highest point of the volcano on the Fogo island. However, if you prefer sun and calm, Chave's beach is the place to go. Their main dishes are Cachupa (a type of stew) and Canjo (a type of soup). In terms of accommodation, Hotel Riu Touareg (4.5 rating stars) and Hotel Morabezas (4.6 rating starts) are ideal for families.

Jordan

Known by its ancient attributes, Jordan is a country situated on the east bank of the Jordan River. Here you can explore Petra's Rock-Cut City in a deep valley or even visit Amman Citadel, an archaeological site with remnants of past civilisations amongst many other attractions. Jerash and Amman are Jordan's most visited cities enriched with history and culture. The climate consists of hot, long and dry summers and cool winters. They are known for their Mansaf (a lamb dish) and Maqluba (meat and rice type of dish). InterContinental Aqaba Hotel (4.5 rating stars) and Petra Palace Hotel (3.4 rating stars) are perfect to accommodate families.

Mozambique

Mozambique is a country located on the southeastern region of Africa, bordering South Africa and Madagascar. It boasts a long coastline, tropical savannah climate with warm temperatures and relatively high humidity. The capital city of Maputo has many entertainment options including the beach, fishing, watersports, restaurants, cinema, theatres and nightclubs. The national language is Portuguese, although English is popular in Maputo too. Most tourists spend a short time in the capital and enjoy the majority of their stay in the outer beaches such as Ponta d’Ouro, Inhambane, Nacala and Pemba. The Mozambican coast is their national treasure, along with their unique cuisine. If you are fortunate enough to visit this amazing place, indulge in the selection of seafood and taste the traditional food like cassava.

Croatia

The Republic of Croatia is at the crossroads of Central and Southeastern Europe. The national language is Croatian, but English and German are widely spoken. The coast has a typically Mediterranean climate consisting of hot, dry, sunny weather during summer, and relatively mild – though sometimes wet – weather in winter. A continental climate exists in the interior of Croatia which means that winters can be pretty cold, with temperatures often falling below 0°C/32°F. Snow is very likely, and can be pretty heavy during the winter. Croatia’s pride is its nature, and 10% of the country is protected. The best way to enjoy this is by camping. They boast fantastic watersports, beautiful islands and gorgeous lighthouses. Each region has its unique dishes and seafood is big throughout the country. Make sure you try the oysters. If you’re adventurous, try the famous Maraschino liqueur.
