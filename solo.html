Solo Travel

New Zealand

New Zealand is a small country, roughly the size of Great Britain and with a population of only 4 million. Summer temperatures average 20-250C which is fantastic for a warm holiday, while winter temperatures can reach -100C, setting the scene for their popular ski season. New Zealanders (informally known as Kiwis) are famous for their friendliness – a huge plus for a solo traveller! It is the perfect destination whether you are backpacking, camping or luxury holidaying. They have options for all budgets. Fun activities include zip-lining, off-road driving and caving, if you are an adventure junkie. If you are looking for a relaxing vacation, there are plenty of hot pools, vineyards, galleries and museums worth exploring. One of New Zealand’s most unique traits is their Māori culture and you can catch one of their Kapa Haka (traditional Māori performing arts). As for food, seafood is king in New Zealand. Other delicious dishes include roast lamb, Māori hāngī, and the timeless fish and chips. If you are Lord of the Rings fan, you will know New Zealand has been dubbed the ‘’Home of Middle-Earth’’.

Japan

Japan is truly timeless, a place where ancient traditions are fused with modern life as if it were the most natural thing in the world. The neon-lit streetscapes of Japan's cities look like sci-fi film sets, even though many of them are decades old. Meanwhile, cities such as Tokyo and Osaka have been adding new architectural wonders that redefine what buildings – and cities – should look like. There's an indelible buzz to these urban centres, with their vibrant street life, 24-hour drinking and dining scenes, and creative hubs that turn out fashion and pop culture trends consumed the world over. Travel is always smooth and efficient, whether you're using the subway to get around or the shinkansen(bullet trains) to go from one city to the next.

Czech Republic

The Czech Republic, in Central Europe, is a country that's known for its ornate castles, native beers and long history. Prague, the capital, is home to grand 9th-century Prague Castle, a preserved medieval old town and statue-lined Charles Bridge. Český Krumlov, a small town in the South Bohemia region, is notable for its wealth of Gothic, Renaissance and Baroque buildings, many of which house restaurants and shops.

Peru

For those who enjoy connecting with nature and love fresh hair, Peru is the right place for you. The Machu Picchu mountains are ideal for a solo get-away. These ancient rocks are one of nature's masterpiece and ideal for a panoramic scenario. Additionally, hiking in these mountains is an excellent experience to keep the body and mind at ease. However, if you are not into hiking, the Paracas National Reserve is notable for their beautiful beaches with a unique view for the vast ocean full of dolphins and sea lions. In Peru the climate is relaxing with high temperatures and little rain falls.
